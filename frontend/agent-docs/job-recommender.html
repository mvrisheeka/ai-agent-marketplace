Overview

The Job Recommender agent takes your resume and optional preferences (location, role type, salary range) and returns a ranked list of job matches with per-role fit scores and explanations.

Recommendations are based on semantic skill matching — not just keyword overlap — so roles that require adjacent skills you haven't listed explicitly can still surface.

Semantic skill matching Role-type filtering Location-aware Match explanations

How it works

1
Submit your resume

The agent extracts your skills, experience level, domain, and educational background.

2
Set preferences (optional)

Specify role type (full-time/intern/contract), location, remote preference, and seniority level.

3
Semantic matching runs

Your profile is matched against the job index using vector similarity + rule-based filters.

4
Ranked results returned

Top matches are returned with fit scores, match reasons, and skill gap highlights per role.

Capabilities

  • Returns 5 role recommendations with clear fit reasons.
  • Includes salary range, missing skills, and next steps.
  • Works independently or as a middle step in A2A flow.
  • Falls back to deterministic recommendations when needed.

Inputs

Required

  • resume_text — Your resume as plain text or uploaded file.

Optional

  • role_type — full-time, internship, or contract.
  • location — City, state, or remote.
  • seniority — entry, mid, or senior. Auto-inferred from resume if not set.
  • top_n — Number of recommendations to return (default: 5, max: 20).

Outputs

RANKED JOB LIST

Top N roles sorted by fit score, each with company, title, location, and role type.

FIT SCORE PER ROLE

0–100 score per recommendation. Broken down into skill match %, experience alignment, and seniority fit.

MATCH REASONS

2–3 sentence explanation of why each role was recommended — which skills matched, what experience was relevant.

SKILL GAPS

Per-role list of skills the job requires that your resume doesn't cover, to guide upskilling.

Tips for best results

Tip: Set seniority explicitly if you're a recent grad or career-switcher — auto-inference from thin resumes can be inaccurate.
  • The more detailed your resume, the better the semantic matching. Bullet points with technologies beat vague summaries.
  • Use top_n: 10 for the first run to explore the range, then narrow with filters.
  • Combine with the Resume Analyzer: improve your resume score first, then re-run recommendations.
