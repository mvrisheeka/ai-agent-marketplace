Overview

The Cover Letter Generator produces a fully personalized cover letter by cross-referencing your resume with the target job description. It highlights relevant experience, mirrors the JD's language, and structures the letter to maximize first-impression impact.

Output is ready-to-send or easy to edit — no more starting from a blank page.

JD-aware personalization Tone control Length options Editable output

How it works

1
Provide your resume

The agent extracts your top experiences, skills, and achievements to draw from.

2
Paste the job description

The agent identifies key requirements, company tone, and role priorities from the JD.

3
Set tone and length

Choose a tone (professional, conversational, etc.) and target length (short / standard / detailed).

4
Cover letter generated

A tailored letter is produced with an opening hook, experience bridge, and call to action.

Skills

Professional Writing Personalization Role Targeting Concise Formatting

Capabilities

  • Generates short recruiter-friendly cover letters.
  • Uses resume or job context to tailor the message.
  • Maintains clean greeting, body, and sign-off structure.
  • Produces copy-ready final output in the marketplace UI.

Inputs

Required

  • resume_text — Your resume as plain text or uploaded file.
  • job_description — The full JD of the role you're applying to.

Optional

  • tone — Writing tone. Options: professional (default), conversational, enthusiastic, concise.
  • length — short (1 para), standard (3 paras, default), or detailed (4–5 paras).
  • company_name — Used to personalize the opening. Auto-extracted from JD if not provided.
  • hiring_manager — Name for the salutation. Defaults to "Hiring Manager" if unknown.

Outputs

COVER LETTER

Full letter in plain text, ready to copy-paste or export. Includes salutation, body paragraphs, and sign-off.

KEY POINTS USED

Bullet list of which resume items were pulled in and why — so you understand the agent's choices.

JD ALIGNMENT NOTES

Short summary of how the letter mirrors the JD's priorities and language, for your reference.

Tone options

professional

Formal and structured. Best for corporate, finance, legal, and enterprise roles.

conversational

Warm and direct. Works well for startups, creative agencies, and product teams.

enthusiastic

High-energy and expressive. Good for early-career applicants and mission-driven orgs.

concise

Minimal and punchy. Ideal when the JD explicitly asks for brevity or a short intro.

Tips for best results

Tip: Provide the full JD — not just the title. The agent uses requirement language to mirror keywords naturally, which improves ATS pass-through.
  • If the company has a clear culture (e.g., very casual Slack-first startup), set tone: conversational to match.
  • Run with length: short first to see the essential pitch, then expand to standard if needed.
  • Edit the output — treat it as a strong first draft, not a final submission. Add personal anecdotes the agent can't know.
  • Pair with the Resume Analyzer to make sure your resume and letter are aligned before applying.
